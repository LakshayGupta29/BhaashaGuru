Atomic Structure - NCERT Chemistry Class 11

The Atom
An atom consists of a nucleus surrounded by electrons. The nucleus contains protons and neutrons.

Subatomic Particles:
1. Proton: Positively charged particle with mass ≈ 1 amu
2. Neutron: Neutral particle with mass ≈ 1 amu
3. Electron: Negatively charged particle with mass ≈ 1/1836 amu

Atomic Number: Number of protons in an atom (denoted by Z)
Mass Number: Sum of protons and neutrons (denoted by A)
Number of neutrons = A - Z

Electron Configuration
Electrons occupy orbitals around the nucleus following the Aufbau principle. The order of filling is:
1s < 2s < 2p < 3s < 3p < 4s < 3d < 4p < 5s < 4d < 5p...

Quantum Numbers:
- Principal quantum number (n): Determines the shell (n = 1, 2, 3, ...)
- Azimuthal quantum number (l): Determines the subshell (s, p, d, f)
- Magnetic quantum number (ml): Determines the orbital
- Spin quantum number (ms): ±1/2

Periodic Table
The periodic table is organized by:
- Periods (horizontal rows): Based on the number of electron shells
- Groups (vertical columns): Based on the number of valence electrons

The periodic table helps predict element properties and reactivity.

Photosynthesis - NCERT Biology Class 10

Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants use sunlight to synthesize foods from carbon dioxide and water. It occurs primarily in the chloroplasts of plant cells.

The equation for photosynthesis:
6CO₂ + 6H₂O + light energy → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂

Photosynthesis occurs in two main stages:

1. Light-Dependent Reactions (in the thylakoid):
- Occurs in the presence of light
- Chlorophyll absorbs light energy
- Water molecules are split (photolysis)
- Produces ATP and NADPH
- Releases oxygen as a byproduct

2. Light-Independent Reactions or Calvin Cycle (in the stroma):
- Occurs with or without light
- Uses ATP and NADPH from light reactions
- Fixes CO₂ into glucose
- Produces glucose molecules

Importance of Photosynthesis:
- Produces oxygen essential for aerobic respiration
- Produces glucose that serves as food and energy source
- Forms the base of most food chains
- Helps maintain oxygen and carbon dioxide balance in the atmosphere
